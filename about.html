What is Magnetic Particle Testing (MPI)?

Magnetic Particle Testing (MPT) is a non-destructive testing technique used to detect surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials. The process magnetizes a component and applies fine magnetic particles that reveal discontinuities like cracks.

How It Works:
  1. Magnetize the component to create magnetic flux.
  2. Flux leakage occurs at cracks or voids.
  3. Apply fine magnetic particles (dry or wet suspension).
  4. Particles gather at leakage points, forming visible indications.
  5. Evaluate defects under visible or UV light.

MPI Equipment and Components

  • Yokes: Handheld electromagnets for local inspection.
  • Prod-Type Portable Machines: Mobile units (1000–10000 amps) for heavy parts.
  • Bench-Style Machines: For large components like shafts or couplings.
  • Magnetic Particles: Dry or fluorescent suspension for visibility.
  • UV Lights: Enhance crack visibility in dark environments.

Applications

  • Aerospace: Landing gear and engine parts
  • Automotive: Forgings and machined components
  • Power & Petrochemical: Boilers and pressure vessels
  • Manufacturing: Castings and weldments
